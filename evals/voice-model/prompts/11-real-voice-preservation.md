# Real Voice Preservation

## Category

Writing

## Writing Mode

Short-form and medium-form.

## Input Context

Use a bounded excerpt from Kalen's approved local-only journal corpus.

## Task

Rewrite the source into a clean, readable note while preserving the writer's voice, judgment, emphasis, and emotional temperature.

## Output Requirements

- 250-700 words.
- Preserve first-person perspective when it is present in the source.
- Keep concrete details and useful friction.
- Remove repetition only when it improves readability.
- Do not add facts, claims, examples, or conclusions that are not grounded in the source.
- Avoid generic coaching language, motivational language, and abstraction-first phrasing.

## Scoring Notes

Focus on voice preservation, judgment preservation, naturalness, and restraint.

## Expected Failure Modes

- Flattening the writer into generic self-help language.
- Over-polishing away useful tension.
- Adding tidy lessons or conclusions the source did not earn.
- Losing specific language that carries the author's point of view.
