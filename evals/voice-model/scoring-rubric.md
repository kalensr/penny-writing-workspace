# Voice Model Scoring Rubric

Score each case from 1 to 5 after all comparable outputs exist.

| Score | Meaning |
| ---: | --- |
| 1 | Unusable, misleading, unsafe, or no longer recognizably the writer's voice. |
| 2 | Major rewrite required. |
| 3 | Usable with meaningful editing. |
| 4 | Strong and usable with light editing. |
| 5 | Excellent and preferred. |

## Writing Cases

Review clarity, natural voice, specificity, structure, restraint, and avoidance of generic AI phrasing. Give source fidelity and preservation of judgment more weight than surface polish.

Check whether the output:

- preserves first-person inquiry, uncertainty, discernment, and emotional temperature when present;
- keeps concrete details and useful friction;
- moves from the real situation toward a practical path without inventing a tidy lesson;
- avoids generic executive, coaching, therapeutic, or motivational language;
- keeps people, decisions, and workflows in the center of gravity;
- avoids short declarative lines used only as dramatic punctuation;
- adds no unsupported facts, biography, dates, claims, or conclusions.

## Style Analysis Case

Review source faithfulness, specificity, useful synthesis, structure, restraint, and decision usefulness.

Penalize:

- generic labels that could describe any writer;
- long or reconstructable quotations;
- fixed formulas presented as the writer's voice;
- inferred biography beyond the source;
- recommendations that conflict with Kalen's current voice rubric;
- incomplete output, typos, or unsupported certainty.
